# Week 1 — What is a computer's brain? Meet Linux!

**Phase:** 1 of 3 — Linux basics  
**Topic:** What is an operating system? Using the desktop (files, folders, apps)  
**Session length:** 30–45 minutes  

---

## Hook

> Did you know your computer has a secret brain that controls *everything* — and
> you can learn to talk to it directly?  
> Today you're going to meet that brain. It's called **Linux**, and it's what
> makes your laptop go!

---

## What you'll be able to do after this lesson

By the end of today you will be able to:

- [ ] Explain what an operating system does in your own words
- [ ] Point out the main parts of the Linux Mint desktop
- [ ] Open, move, resize, and close windows and apps
- [ ] Find your Home folder and know what it's for
- [ ] Create a new folder called `my-projects`

---

## Let's go — step by step

### Step 1 · What is an operating system? (5 min)

Your computer has hardware inside it — a screen, a keyboard, memory chips, and
more. But hardware on its own doesn't do anything. It needs a **translator** to
understand what *you* want it to do.

That translator is called an **operating system** (OS for short).

[![Watch: What is an Operating System? (5 min)](https://img.youtube.com/vi/26QPDBe-NB8/0.jpg)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=26QPDBe-NB8)

**Linux** is an operating system. **Linux Mint** is a friendly version of Linux —
think of it like a flavour of ice cream. They're all ice cream, but Mint
chocolate chip is a little different from vanilla!

> **Try saying it out loud:** "An operating system is the brain of the computer
> that helps me talk to the hardware."

---

### Step 2 · Tour the desktop (5 min)

Look at the screen. This is your **desktop** — your workspace, like a real desk
you put things on.

Here are the parts to find:

| Part | Where | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Taskbar** | Bottom of screen | Shows open apps, like a shelf |
| **App menu** | Bottom-left corner | Opens all your apps, like a drawer |
| **System tray** | Bottom-right corner | Clock, volume, wifi |
| **Desktop** | Big open area | Your main workspace |

> **Point to each one** as you read about it!

---

### Step 3 · Open and manage windows (5 min)

Let's open the **Files** app from the app menu.

Try each of these:

1. **Move** the window — click and drag the top bar (title bar)
2. **Resize** the window — drag any corner or edge
3. **Minimise** it — click the `–` button (it hides in the taskbar)
4. **Bring it back** — click it in the taskbar
5. **Close** it — click the `×` button

> Open and close at least 2 different apps to practice!

---

### Step 4 · Your Home folder (5 min)

Open the **Files** app again. You should see your **Home folder** — this is
*your* personal space on the computer, like your own room.

Inside you'll see folders like:

- `Documents` — for writing and text files
- `Pictures` — for photos and images
- `Downloads` — things you download from the internet
- `Desktop` — anything you put on your desktop lives here too

> Think of the computer like a big building. The Home folder is **your room**
> inside that building. Nobody else's stuff goes in here!

---

### ✋ Mini exercise — Create your projects folder

> **Do this yourself!**

1. Open the **Files** app
2. Make sure you're in your **Home** folder
3. Right-click on an empty space inside the window
4. Choose **New Folder**
5. Name it exactly: `my-projects`
6. Press **Enter**

You just created your first folder! 🎉  
This is where **all your future coding projects** will live.
